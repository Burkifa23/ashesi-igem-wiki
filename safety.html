Zero-Escape

Containment.

Autonomous biosafety protocols designed to ensure absolute environmental isolation.

TIER 1 // MACROSCOPIC

Physical Isolation

Standard glass bioreactors shatter. To prevent environmental leakage during transport or seismic events, the culture is housed within aerospace-grade Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) bags. This material is biologically inert, mechanically flexible, and highly resistant to puncture.

TIER 2 // GENETIC

Semantic Containment

If physical containment fails, the hardware relies on Xenobiology. We engineered a strict synthetic auxotrophy. The biological processors cannot synthesize essential survival proteins without a non-canonical amino acid (p-acetylphenylalanine) exclusively provided by the OS. In the wild, they starve instantly.

TIER 3 // ALGORITHMIC

Optogenetic Kill-Switch

The ultimate failsafe. The bioreactor interior is bathed in red light. If the casing is cracked, exposing the culture to natural ambient blue light ($\lambda$ = 470 nm), the pBlind promoter immediately triggers the expression of Holin/Endolysin, physically rupturing the cell membrane in seconds.
