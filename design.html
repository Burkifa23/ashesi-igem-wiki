SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The Genetic

Blueprint.

Drafting the topology of an orthogonal, $\sigma^{54}$-dependent biological processor.

MODULE 01

The Sensor Array

To compute the environment, the system requires discrete inputs. We utilized the native E. coli heat-shock promoter ($P_{htpG}$) to detect thermal drift above 37°C, and a highly tuned $P_{arsR}$ promoter to detect bioavailable heavy metals.

INPUT AThermal Drift (>37°C)
INPUT BArsenic Toxicity (>10ppb)
MODULE 02

The Logic Processor

Standard repressors leak under stress. We designed a strict AND gate utilizing the HrpR and HrpS enhancer-binding proteins from P. syringae. Neither can actuate transcription alone. They must physically bind together into a hetero-hexamer to hydrolyze ATP and open the promoter.

MECHANISMHetero-hexameric Assembly
PROMOTER$\sigma^{54}$-dependent ($P_{hrpL}$)
MODULE 03

The Actuator

Upon successful computation of both stress states (TRUE AND TRUE), the $\sigma^{54}$ polymerase complex is unlocked. The circuit actuates our reporter gene, triggering a massive downstream synthesis of GFP for visual telemetry.
