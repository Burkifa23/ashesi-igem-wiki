The Hypoxic Cascade

The grid fails. Impellers and spargers die. Because the solubility of molecular oxygen in aqueous media is exceptionally low, the dense cellular biomass consumes all residual dissolved oxygen in under 30 seconds. The culture is plunged into strict hypoxia.

T+ 01:15:00

Mixed-Acid Fermentation

The autophosphorylated ArcA~P regulator represses terminal oxidases. Surviving the ATP deficit requires forcing carbon flux through fermentative pathways. The massive, immediate upregulation of pflAB floods the media with toxic acetate and lactate.

T+ 03:00:00

PMF Collapse

The buffering capacity of the continuous-culture media is overwhelmed. The pH plummets toward 4.0. Lipophilic organic acids diffuse across the E. coli membrane, dissociating in the cytoplasm and entirely collapsing the Proton Motive Force required for ATP synthesis.

T+ 04:00:01 // GRID RESTORED

The Re-Oxygenation Crisis.

Counterintuitively, the most biologically destructive phase is the moment power returns. The sudden blast of pure air into a hypoxic liquid backed up with reduced electron carriers generates a massive burst of Reactive Oxygen Species ($O_2^{\bullet -}$, $H_2O_2$).

This oxidative shock induces catastrophic lipid peroxidation and fractures genomic DNA via the Fenton reaction, permanently destroying batch viability.
