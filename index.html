In-Situ Resource Utilization

NASA relies on biological computers for deep space missions. Transporting abiotic plastics to Mars imposes an insurmountable mass penalty. The solution is biological ISRU: utilizing lithoautotrophic bacteria to synthesize bio-polymers offline, in the harshest environments conceivable.

The Terrestrial Translation

The infrastructural vacuum of a Martian colony perfectly mirrors the logistical realities of ultra-rural West Africa. Commercial grids in Ghana experience severe voltage sags under 207V for 22% of their operational time. If a biological system can survive Mars, it can survive the terrestrial grid.

Programming the Cell

We strip away the cloud and the hardware. By engineering synthetic plasmids into *E. coli*, we create orthogonal AND logic gates. The cell itself acts as the microprocessor, calculating environmental toxicity and thermal drift natively.

Grid restoration blasts pure air into a hypoxic liquid. The resulting burst of Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) fractures genomic DNA via the Fenton reaction. The recovery is more lethal than the blackout.

03 // BIOLOGICAL COMPUTING

HrpR/HrpS Logic Gates

We program the *E. coli* chassis to autonomously calculate environmental inputs. By refactoring the Pseudomonas syringae Type III secretion system, we engineered a mathematically robust AND gate.

Input A: Arsenic Toxicity ($P_{arsR}$)

Bioavailable arsenite allosterically binds the ArsR repressor, dissociating it from the operator. This de-represses the promoter, synthesizing the HrpR monomer.

Input B: Thermal Drift (cI857)

The mutated viral repressor cI857 is thermolabile. As grid failures cause bioreactor temps to drift $\ge$ 37°C, the repressor denatures, synthesizing the HrpS monomer.

Output: Actuation ($P_{hrpL}$)

Only when both inputs are strictly TRUE do HrpR and HrpS stoichiometrically bind into a hetero-hexameric complex, driving the $P_{hrpL}$ promoter to actuate the failsafe response (Visualized here as GFP).

04 // SYSTEM MODELING

Transfer Functions

To ensure the Bio-Foundry does not falsely actuate during transcriptomic chaos, the HrpR/HrpS gate must operate digitally. We parameterized the steady-state input-output response using a multi-variable Hill equation.

[G] = [G]max * ( [R]^nR / (Kr^nR + [R]^nR) ) * ( [S]^nS / (Ks^nS + [S]^nS) )
  • Coefficients: nR = 2.381, nS = 1.835
  • Constants: Kr = 206.1, Ks = 3135
  • Result: The Hill coefficients (>1) confirm highly cooperative binding, creating a sharp, digital transition from OFF to ON, eliminating noisy intermediate states.

Hardware Imperatives

Translating aerospace In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) to West African grid instability requires abandoning traditional CSTR bioreactors and engineering metabolic shock absorbers.

01. FEP Bioreactor Bags

REF: ISS BioNutrients

Standard bioreactors fail instantly without power. We adopt NASA's Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) flexible bags. Their exceptionally high gas permeability maintains passive oxygen transfer during blackout-induced impeller failures.

02. ROS Scavenging

REF: Re-Oxygenation Crisis

When the grid recovers, the sudden blast of $O_2$ into a hypoxic culture generates lethal superoxide anions. Our chassis constitutively overexpresses sodA and katG to neutralize the Fenton reaction and survive the transient.

03. Low-Copy Actuation

REF: pSC101 Backbone

Deploying the logic output on a high-copy plasmid leads to leaky actuation during voltage sags. We isolate the output module on a strictly low-copy (pSC101) plasmid, ensuring absolute silence until strictly commanded.
