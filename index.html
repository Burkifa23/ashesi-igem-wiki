Project 2025

Cultivating Protein.
Redefining Meat.

We are using cellular agriculture to engineer sustainable, slaughter-free meat tailored for the African continent. High-quality protein without the environmental cost of traditional livestock.

The Protein Deficit

As population growth accelerates across West Africa, traditional livestock farming is draining our water resources and accelerating deforestation. We need a localized, scalable biomanufacturing solution to ensure future food security.

Our Engineering Approach

01

Immortalized Cell Lines

We are engineering local bovine and poultry cell lines to proliferate indefinitely in suspension, removing the need for continuous animal biopsies and lowering long-term production costs.

02

Plant-Based Scaffolding

Cultured cells need a structure to form textured meat. We are utilizing abundant local crops like cassava and yam starch to create edible, 3D bio-printed scaffolds for the cells to attach to.

03

Serum-Free Media

Replacing controversial Fetal Bovine Serum (FBS) with a cost-effective, recombinantly produced growth media tailored specifically for our engineered cell lines.

Integrated Human Practices

Technology alone cannot change diets. Our project is deeply integrated with local stakeholders to ensure cultural acceptance and economic viability.

  • Farmer Integration: Exploring how traditional farmers can pivot to supplying agricultural inputs for bio-scaffolding.
  • Consumer Perception: Conducting comprehensive surveys across Ghana to understand the ethical and culinary acceptance of lab-grown meat.
  • Regulatory Frameworks: Partnering with local food safety authorities to draft the first guidelines for cellular agriculture in West Africa.
